Child Safety Standards

Last updated: 15 April 2026

Kindred is committed to the safety and protection of children. This page sets out the standards we uphold to prevent, detect, and respond to child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), including child sexual abuse material (CSAM), across our services.

1. Age Requirement

Kindred is not intended for users under the age of 18. Our sign-up flow enforces a date-of-birth check, and accounts created by anyone under 18 are removed when detected. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we learn that we have collected data from a minor, we delete it promptly and terminate the account.

2. Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on Kindred, and will result in immediate account termination and, where applicable, referral to law enforcement:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any kind — images, video, audio, drawings, text, or AI-generated content depicting the sexual abuse or exploitation of minors.
  • Grooming, solicitation, or sexual communication directed at or involving minors.
  • Sharing or requesting sexual content involving minors, or content that sexualises minors.
  • Attempting to meet, contact, or arrange meetings with users under 18.
  • Sharing personal information, contact details, or images of minors without the verified consent of a parent or legal guardian.
  • Trafficking, exploitation, or endangerment of minors in any form.

3. In-App Reporting

Every user profile, listing, and conversation in Kindred includes a report function. Users can flag content or accounts they believe violate our child safety standards. Reports are triaged by our moderation team within 24 hours.

Reports relating to CSAE or CSAM are escalated immediately and prioritised above all other reports. Users can also contact our safety team directly at support@kindred.nz with the subject "Child Safety" for urgent matters.

4. Moderation and Enforcement

Our moderation approach combines:

  • Automated detection — image hashing against known CSAM hash databases, keyword filters, and behavioural signals for grooming patterns.
  • Human review — all reports involving potential CSAE are reviewed by trained moderators.
  • Account action — confirmed violations result in permanent account termination, device/IP blocking, and removal of all associated content.
  • Preservation — relevant evidence (account data, content, metadata) is preserved in line with law enforcement requests and applicable retention laws.

5. Reporting to Authorities

Kindred complies with all relevant child safety laws in the jurisdictions where we operate. This includes:

  • New Zealand — reporting suspected CSAM to the Department of Internal Affairs (DIA) and, where applicable, NZ Police. We cooperate with the Digital Child Exploitation Filtering System and the Films, Videos, and Publications Classification Act 1993.
  • International — where required, reporting to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline or the equivalent national reporting body for the user's country.

We respond to valid legal process and emergency disclosure requests from law enforcement promptly.

6. Training and Internal Standards

All moderators who review child safety reports complete training on identifying CSAE, trauma-informed handling of material, legal reporting obligations, and secure evidence preservation. Training is refreshed at least annually and on material policy change.

7. Review and Updates

These standards are reviewed at least annually, or whenever there is a material change to our product, moderation tooling, or legal environment. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.

8. Contact

Child safety point of contact: support@kindred.nz

For urgent CSAE concerns, please also contact your local law enforcement. In New Zealand, call 111 in an emergency, or report online to the Department of Internal Affairs at dia.govt.nz.
